Rudy Castellani MD

His research is dedicated to the study of neurodegenerative disease pathogenesis, including Alzheimer’s disease and so-called prion diseases. He has extensively characterized sporadic Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease in terms of clinical phenotype, pathological alterations, prion chemistry, and molecular genetics, and have found that specific features of the PrPsc molecule are remarkable predictors of phenotype.

Further, Dr. Castellani has recently characterized a form of human prion disease characterized by “protease-sensitive PrPsc,” expanding the spectrum of disease and drawing even closer overlap with more common neurodegenerative diseases. His studies on Alzheimer’s disease relate to early events in disease progression; as such, he has  found a number of adducts related to oxidative stress in vulnerable, but pathologically normal, neurons in Alzheimer’s disease, as well as in control brains several decades prior to the onset of neurodegeneration.

Micky Collins PhD

Michael (Micky) Collins, Ph.D., a nationally renowned sports concussion clinician and researcher, joined the UPMC Sports Medicine Concussion Program as assistant director when the program was established in September, 2000, under the direction of Dr. Mark Lovell. In 1999, Dr. Collins was the author of two major multi-site studies involving the effects of concussion and return-to-play evaluation methods. These studies were published in the Journal of the American Medical Association (JAMA).

Dr. Collins continues to administer and oversee clinical programs for concussion management for sports teams at several major colleges, universities, and high schools throughout the country. He is a clinical consultant to Major League Baseball, the New Zealand Rugby Union, and to several amateur and professional sports organizations both nationally and internationally.

Dr. Collins is a co-developer of ImPACT, a computerized neuropsychological testing system that evaluates the severity of concussion in athletes and provides a more accurate determination of when an athlete can safely return to sports following a concussion. Dr. Collins received his bachelor's degree in psychology and biology at the University of Southern Maine in 1991, his master's degree in psychology at Michigan State University in 1995, and his doctorate degree in clinical psychology at Michigan State in 1998. He subsequently completed a clinical neuropsychology internship at the University of Florida and a neuropsychology residency at the Henry Ford
Health System in Detroit.

He has published numerous articles in sports medicine and other medical journals and is a frequently invited presenter on the topic of sports concussions at national and international meetings. He has co-authored a sports concussion textbook and is a reviewer for JAMA. A two-year winner of the New England Pepsi-Cola Scholar/Athlete of the Year Award, Dr. Collins played for the University of Southern Maine in the 1989 NCAA Baseball College World Series.

Kimberly Peer EdD

Dr. Peer is an Associate Professor at Kent State University and holds an adjunct appointment at Northeast Ohio College of Medicine and KSU College of Podiatric Medicine. She holds a Doctorate in Higher Education Administration with a Cognate in Health Care Management.  Kimberly Serves as the Editor-in-Chief for the Athletic Training Education Journal and serves on the Commission on Accreditation of Athletic Training Education Ethics Committee as well as the NATA Committee on Professional Ethics. Her national contributions include service to the BOC, NATA, JAT and REF in multiple capacities.  Her statewide service includes the Governor’s appointment to the Ohio licensure board and over 12 years of service to the OATA.

Peer received the NATA Fellow, Most Distinguished Athletic Trainer, and Outstanding Service Awards, BOC Dan Libera Service Award, GLATA Outstanding Educator Award and OATA Hall of Fame and Linda Weber Daniel Outstanding Mentor Awards. Kimberly has been lauded with other national, regional, and state level awards for her contributions to the profession and athletic training education. Nicole ReamsMD

Nicole Reams is a fellowship-trained Sports Neurologist at NorthShore University Health System in Chicago, IL and serves there as the Associate Director of the Sports Concussion Program. She is the independent neurological consultant for the Chicago Bears, Chicago Wolves, Chicago Fire, Chicago Force, and  Northwestern University Athletics.  

She also serves as the medical director for United States Intercollegiate Boxing Association. She
works with athletes of all ages and partners with many local high schools in developing concussion policy. Dr. Reams has spoken nationally on topics of acute concussion management and Postconcussion Syndrome and has ongoing research interests in Traumatic Encephalopathy Syndrome and other possible long term sequelae of head trauma.

Sean Rose MD

Sean Rose is a board-certified pediatric neurologist, specializing in sports concussion evaluation and treatment, as well as management of other neurological disorders in athletes. He is the Director of Clinical Research and a Regional Director at The Sports Neurology Clinic.

Dr. Rose serves as a covering physician for the U.S. Ski and Snowboard Association. His research interests include the acute, chronic, and long-term neurologic effects of concussion and repetitive sub-concussive hits. Among other research, he is directing the Brighton Football Study, a four year in-depth look at head impacts and potential neurocognitive effects in high school and youth league players.

Dr. Rose completed a Sports Neurology fellowship at The Sports Neurology Clinic at The CORE Institute®. He completed his residency in Child Neurology at Nationwide Children’s Hospital – The Ohio State University. He
obtained his medical degree from the University of Virginia School of Medicine, and completed his undergraduate degree at the University of Notre Dame. Dr. Rose is a member of the American Academy of Neurology (AAN) and the Child Neurology Society (CNS). He is board certified in Neurology with Special Qualification in Child Neurology.

Anthony Savino MD

Dr. Anthony Savino is a board-certified neurologist and a fellow at The Sports Neurology Clinic at The CORE Institute®, specializing in the treatment of concussion, post-concussion syndrome and neurological disorders in athletes.  His educational background includes undergraduate studies at the University of Colorado, medical school at Rush Medical College in Chicago, internship at Loyola University Medical Center and neurology residency at Boston University Medical Center.

Dr. Savino’s research interests include autonomic dysfunction in concussion and the treatment of post-concussion syndrome. He is currently participating in a four-year collaborative research study of head trauma in local youth and high school football players. The study examines the potential
chronic results of brain trauma by measuring head impacts in real-time with helmet sensors and conducting extensive brain function tests before and after each football season.

Kathy J. Turpin PhD

As the Vice President of Sport Drug Testing at The National Center for Drug Free Sport (Drug Free Sport), Dr. Kathy Turpin uses her expertise and experience in drug testing to provide institutions with information that empowers student-athletes and administrators to make decisions that will ensure the success of their programs.

Since joining Drug Free Sport in 2008, her professional history includes departmental supervision of more than 300 drug testing collegiate clients across the country and more than 25 years as a collegiate athletics administrator and coach. Dr. Turpin was also a four-year starter on the University of Missouri-Columbia women’s basketball team and the first female to be inducted into the Culver-Stockton College Athletic Hall of Fame for her successful ten-year stint as the Head Women’s Basketball Coach.

A frequent presenter on NCAA compliance and sport drug testing/educational issues, she has served on numerous conference and NCAA committees, including a two-year term as chair of the NCAA Division II Women’s Basketball Committee. Dr. Turpin possesses a Bachelor’s degree from the University of Missouri-Columbia, a master’s degree in sport sociology from Western Illinois University and a Ph.D. in leadership and higher education administration from Barry University (FL).

Rod Walter PhD

An innovative professional, Dr. Rod Walters has enjoyed a storied career as a collegiate athletic trainer. A native of Fayetteville, North Carolina, Walters has long been active in the field of athletic training since his graduation from Appalachian State University in 1980, where he received his undergraduate and master's degrees. In 1988, Walters received his Doctor of Arts degree in Physical Education from Middle Tennessee State University. Walters was Head Athletic Trainer at Lenoir-Rhyne College (N.C.) for five years before joining the staff at Appalachian State. Following his work as Head Athletic Trainer at Appalachian State, Dr. Walters moved to the Uni¬versity of South Carolina, a member of the nation's top athletic league in the Southeastern Confer¬ence, to take a similar position in 1990.

While at the University of South Carolina Dr. Walters was elevated to the position of Assistant Athletic Director for Sports Medicine where he oversaw the health care and wellness for the university's athletes. A member of the National Athletic Trainers Association, Dr. Walters served on their Board of Directors from 1997 to 2003. He received the NATA's Most Distinguished Athletic Trainer in 2003 and was inducted into the NATA's Hall of Fame in 2005. He is also a member of the American Orthopaedic Society for Sports Medicine. Most recently, Dr. Walters has brought his expertise across the country with his athletic training seminars.
